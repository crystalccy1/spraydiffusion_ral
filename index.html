Speech-Driven Gesture Generation via Conditional Flow Matching with Masked Training and Constrained Sampling

Demo on Github

Abstract

The ability to perform co‐speech gestures is essential for robots with human‐like appearances. In this paper, we present a novel conditional flow matching-based generative model to generate co‐speech gestures from speech input. Moreover, we propose a masked training strategy and the corresponding masked generation algorithm to ensure smooth transitions in sequential gesture movements when processing segmented, long speech inputs. Furthermore, masked generation inherently supports general purpose partial gesture completion. To enable direct deployment on humanoid robots, we introduce constrained sampling algorithm which enforces physical limits during generation, thereby avoiding hard post‐processing (e.g., clamping) that may distort intended gestures. User study shows that our model outperforms existing baselines in gesture naturalness and is competitive in speech appropriateness. Our experiments confirm the efficacy of masked generation and constrained sampling.
